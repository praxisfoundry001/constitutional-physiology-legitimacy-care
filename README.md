# Constitutional Physiology: Measuring Legitimacy as Care in a Living Civic Body (White Paper v1.15)

## What this is
This is an early Constitutional Physiology white paper preserved as an origin-layer document in the development of the broader Structural Flow civic project.

## Why it matters
It shows where the civic and constitutional side of the work began: the Republic treated as a living body whose health depends on care, proportionality, memory, autonomy, and renewal.

## What it argues
The paper develops a physiological framing of constitutional order in which legitimacy appears as the pulse of a living civic body and constitutional health can be read through functions such as circulation, regulation, autonomy, cognition, autophagy, and replication.

## What this is not
- Not the final canonical formulation of the later civic field work
- Not a retrofitted current-canon artifact
- Not a replacement for constitutional doctrine or legal analysis
- Not a full Structural Flow civic closure document

## How to read it
Read it as an origin-layer white paper preserved largely in its earlier form. A Historical Position Note in the artifact explains how it sits relative to the later canon.
